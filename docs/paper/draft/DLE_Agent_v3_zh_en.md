#  DLE-Agent: A Dual-Level Evolution Framework for Stateful AI Research Agents

## Abstract

AI research agents have recently shown strong promise in automating machine learning engineering. However, most existing systems remain cognitively stateless: they search over candidate code solutions without explicitly maintaining and updating an evolving understanding of the task. As a result, they often rely on shallow trial-and-error, accumulate little task-specific knowledge across steps, and struggle to explore efficiently on challenging multi-step problems. To address this limitation, we introduce DLE-Agent, a framework for stateful AI research agents based on dual-level evolution. At the outer level, DLE-Agent evolves research branches by selecting, advancing, and revisiting candidate lines of investigation according to their promise. At the inner level, it evolves a structured cognitive state within each branch, enabling the agent to accumulate task understanding, working hypotheses, learned failure patterns, search preferences, and self-assessed confidence over time. Rather than treating memory as passive storage or reflection as a one-step repair tool, DLE-Agent updates the cognitive state using multi-dimensional execution feedback, including performance trends, error types, novelty, and confidence signals. The updated state then guides subsequent operator selection and solution generation within the current branch, while outer-level branch evolution determines which line of investigation to pursue next. In this way, research is transformed from blind traversal of the solution space into a closed-loop process in which branch-level exploration and cognitive-state refinement continually reinforce one another. On AIRS-Bench across 20 cross-domain tasks, DLE-Agent achieves an average normalized score of 0.670 with a 50-step budget, outperforming AIRA-dojo’s 0.402 obtained with 200 steps and 10 seeds. Beyond aggregate performance, we conduct causal state intervention experiments by replacing the evolved cognitive state with blank states, unrelated task states, or frozen snapshots. These interventions show that the cognitive state carries task-specific information and causally improves downstream decisions, rather than merely serving as auxiliary prompt context. The gains are especially pronounced on challenging multi-step tasks, suggesting that explicit dual-level evolution over research branches and cognitive states is a key ingredient for building more capable AI research agents

## 摘要

AI研究智能体近年来在自动化机器学习工程方面展现出强大潜力。然而，现有系统大多在认知层面是无状态的：它们在候选代码方案空间中搜索，却不显式地维护和更新对任务的演化理解。因此，这些系统往往依赖浅层的试错，在各步骤之间几乎不积累任务特定的知识，并且在具有挑战性的多步问题上难以高效探索。为解决这一局限，我们提出了 DLE-Agent——一个基于双层演化的有状态AI研究智能体框架。在外层，DLE-Agent通过选择、推进和回溯候选研究分支来演化研究方向，根据各分支的潜力决定下一步探索路径。在内层，DLE-Agent在每个分支内部演化一个结构化的认知状态，使智能体能够随时间积累任务理解、工作假设、已习得的失败模式、搜索偏好以及自评置信度。与将记忆视为被动存储或将反思视为一次性修复工具的做法不同，DLE-Agent 利用多维执行反馈来更新认知状态，这些反馈包括性能趋势、错误类型、新颖性和置信度信号。更新后的状态随即引导当前分支内的后续算子选择和方案生成，而外层的分支演化则决定下一步追究哪条研究路线。通过这种方式，研究过程从解空间的盲目遍历，转变为分支级探索与认知状态精炼持续相互强化的闭环过程。在 AIRS-Bench 的 20 个跨领域任务上，DLE-Agent 以 30 步预算取得了 0.670 的平均归一化得分，超越了 AIRA-dojo 在 200 步、10 个种子条件下获得的0.402。在总体性能之外，我们还进行了因果状态干预实验：用空白状态、无关任务状态或冻结快照替换演化后的认知状态。这些干预表明，认知状态承载了任务特定的信息，并因果性地改善了下游决策，而非仅仅充当辅助的提示上下文。这种增益在具有挑战性的多步任务上尤为显著，表明对研究分支和认知状态进行显式的双层演化，是构建更强大AI研究智能体的关键要素


近年来，AI研究智能体在自动化机器学习工程方面展现出强大潜力。然而，现有大多数系统仍然是“认知无状态”的：它们在候选解空间中进行搜索，却未能显式维护和持续更新对任务的结构化理解。因此，这类方法往往依赖浅层的试错过程，难以积累任务相关经验，并在复杂多步骤问题中表现出较低的探索效率。
为了解决这一问题，我们提出 DLE-Agent，一种将自动化科研过程建模为**双层优化问题（dual-level optimization）**的有状态研究智能体框架。在外层，智能体在多个候选研究路径（research branches）之间进行动态选择与资源分配，从而优先探索更具潜力的研究方向；在内层，每一条路径都维护一个结构化的认知状态𝑆，用于编码任务理解、工作假设、失败模式以及置信度等信息。
